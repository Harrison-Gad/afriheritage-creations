Traditional Games

Oware

Oware is a strategy game of the Mancala family, played in West Africa and the Caribbean. It's a game of skill and calculation, where players aim to capture more seeds than their opponent.

Cultural Value: Oware teaches strategic thinking and is often used to encourage social interaction and sportsmanship among youth.

Ampe

Ampe is a lively game of jumping, clapping, and rhythm, popular among children in Ghana and neighboring countries. It's a game that requires energy, coordination, and a bit of luck.

Cultural Value: Ampe promotes physical activity, rhythm, and social bonding. It's often played during festivals and community gatherings.

Ludo

While Ludo has origins in India, it has been widely adopted and Africanized across the continent. It's a simple race game based on sheer luck, making it fun for all ages.

Cultural Value: In many African homes, Ludo is a family-favorite, bringing together different generations for a time of laughter and friendly competition.
